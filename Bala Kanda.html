Bala-Kanda

Dashratha, the emperor of Kosala kingdom, had three wives:elder one was Kaushalya,then Kaikay and Sumitra. King Dashratha had no child. He was very anxious. Seeing his condidtion, his kul-guru Vashishtha made them perform a yajna called Putrakameshthi Yajna. After doing this yajna, Rama was born to Kaushalya, Bharata to Kaikay and Lakshmana and Shatrughna to Sumitra. When they were grown, Rama and Lakshmana were asked by their father to accompany Rishi Vishwamitra to the forests to fight the demons Tadka, Maricha and Subahu. Vishwamitra gave many invinsible powers to Rama and Lakshmana. After killing the demons, Rishivar was requested by King Janaka to come to Janakapuri for the Swayamvara of her lovely daughter, Sita. The condition of marriage was that person needs to break the holy bow of Lord Shiva. Rama married Sita after breaking the bow of Shiva. After their marriage, King Dashratha decided to marry his rest three sons to King Janaka's rest three daughters. So, Bharata was married to Mandavi, Lakshmana to Urmilla and Shatrughna to Shrutkirti. During this time, both Janaka and Dashratha had opened their treasures for the people. It was a very special time.
